O gentle pilgrim, thou dost inquire of the chief city of fair France! Verily, tis Paris, that noble and bright citadel, where rivers Seine do flow full fair and maidens chanson sweet. Many a tale hath been spake of yond city, from palaces gleaming to gardens full green. There, lords and ladies gather in great mirth, and all the world doth marvel at her splendor. Paris, soothly, is her name!
